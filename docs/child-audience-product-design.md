# Child audience product design

> **Stub** — cohort ideas seeded; full playbook in the repo for agents and organizers who clone or fork.

Community festivals often label everything **“family-friendly.”** That hides incompatible needs: a stroller-bound infant, a parallel-play toddler, a school-age contest entrant, a sensory-sensitive sibling, and a teen who wants boba and photos are **different products** sharing one field.

**Hyper-focus** means naming **cohorts**, assigning **owners**, and designing **spatial + programming + product** per cohort — not one undifferentiated kids zone.

**Fit annotations** (menu model): each activation gets labeled like a food allergen line — *stroller sightline*, *parallel play*, *quiet*, *loud/drums* — so parents filter for **intentional design**, not a single “family-friendly” claim.

---

## Why this lives on Festival Network

LNY and Autumn capstones both run kid-forward programming (Children's Village, lantern walk, school contests). The same design gaps recur:

- Hand-held procession lanterns with **no stroller-mount option**
- Stage-height spectacle with **nothing at stroller sightline**
- One “family” label with **no sensory or mobility metadata**

## Design axes

Every cohort should be specified on:

1. **Mobility** — stroller, walking toddler, wheelchair, independent  
2. **Engagement mode** — visual field only · parallel play · hands-on · passive viewing  
3. **Parent surplus** — logistics · rest · joint delight · shareable moment  
4. **Sensory** — quiet · moderate · loud — with opt-out  
5. **Exit** — nap window · leave-anytime · hard stop

## Cohort stubs (seed list)

| ID | Cohort | One-line design note |
|----|--------|----------------------|
| **C0** | Stroller-bound / pre-walking (≈0–2) | **Crib-mobile logic** — long-hanging, glittery décor at **low height**; may not “play” or “watch a show” but needs a calm visual field |
| **C1** | Walking toddler (≈2–4) | Parallel play, 10–15 min stations, bounded zones — same low décor helps |
| **C2** | Preschool / young child (≈4–6) | Hands-on beats spectacle; short show blocks |
| **C3** | School age (≈6–12) | Contest, craft → procession, gallery pride |
| **C4** | Teen (≈13–17) | Separate map pin — food, photo, evening — **peer discoverability + facilitated crew** when arrived with family (not only with friends) |
| **SN-A** | Autism / sensory-sensitive | Quiet corral, published sensory notes, predictable schedule — any age |
| **SN-D** | Developmentally delayed | Engagement ≠ chronological age; facilitator ratio; sibling-safe alternatives |
| **SN-W** | Wheelchair / mobility device | Not the same as “stroller-friendly”; path audit, table height, lane width |

Special-needs rows **cross-cut** age bands — one family may need C1 + SN-A simultaneously.

## Fit annotations (menu model)

**Not certification tiers** — per-activation labels, like **gluten-free** or **halal** on a food menu:

| Instead of… | Publish… |
|-------------|----------|
| “Family-friendly festival” | Tag each block: `hands-on`, `stroller-sightline`, `sensory-loud`, `early-exit-ok` |
| One kids zone | Map pins with **combo tags** (e.g. craft station: `hands-on` + `school-age` + `nap-shade`) |
| Silent gaps | Untagged = **unknown fit**, not “all ages” |

Full slug vocabulary (stub): [playbook § Fit annotations](https://github.com/cPALSs/festival-network/blob/main/assets/shared/playbooks/child-cohort-product-design.md#fit-annotations-menu-model)

## Teens arrived with family (peer discoverability)

Meet-back at 6:30 solves **parent** worry, not **teen** loneliness. A teen with friends gets a third place; a teen with family can feel **physically present, socially unmoored** — especially if they cannot tell whether peers are on the field.

**Three stacked layers** (full detail in [playbook § C4](https://github.com/cPALSs/festival-network/blob/main/assets/shared/playbooks/child-cohort-product-design.md#family-attendance-isolation--peer-discoverability)):

1. **Pre-event signal** — Affiliated meetups on the website (VSA @ Teen Plaza 6:30); teen-forward share assets; named crews create ambient density  
2. **On-site opt-in presence** — Optional school affiliation counts, event-scoped chat QR, open crew board — proof peers exist, not matchmaking  
3. **Facilitated crew as default** — 15–25 min crew calls when the friend graph did not show; autonomy **after** the on-ramp  

**Empty Teen Plaza** is worse than no plaza — schedule density (crew calls + club meetups) so furniture is never dead at 5 PM.

## Example: stroller height & procession props

**Observation:** Stroller-bound babies often neither play nor spectate in the adult sense — but a **mobile-like** visual field (slow motion, light catch) at stroller height can work.

**Spatial:** Install décor for **24–36 in sightlines**, not only stage height. Walking toddlers benefit from the same lower plane.

**Product gap:** Lanterns and light strings are almost always **hand-held**. Mardi Gras culture decorates **strollers and wagons** for parades — a precedent for **clip-on / rail-mount** LNY and Mid-Autumn procession kits. Network backlog: spec a stroller-mount lantern SKU.

## Hyper-focus staffing (stub)

One “Children's Village director” is insufficient when products differ:

- **C0 lead** — stroller paths, low décor, mountable-light pilot  
- **C1 lead** — toddler corral, parallel play  
- **C2–C3 lead** — craft pipeline, shows, contest  
- **C4 liaison** — teen pin, evening layer  
- **Access lead** — SN-* cross-cut, map metadata, quiet zone  

Small festivals may combine people; **specs stay split by cohort**.
